The Camino de la Plata, or the Silver Route, is a historic and storied pilgrimage path that meanders through the heart of Spain.

Starting in Seville, this ancient Roman road traverses northward across the Iberian Peninsula, stretching over 1,000 kilometers. It's marked by diverse landscapes, from verdant valleys to arid plains, leading pilgrims through cultural treasures, medieval towns, and Roman ruins.

The Camino de la Plata culminates in the sacred city of Santiago de Compostela, where the reputed tomb of Saint James the Apostle beckons travelers. Each year, it calls to those seeking spiritual growth, adventure, and the embrace of Spain's rich heritage.
